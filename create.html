The scenario focuses on the Allied drives up the Solomon Islands and New Guinea. The game features a damaged but still powerful Imperial Japanese Navy (IJN) poised to slow and potentially stop the Allied advance. The game starts on the eve of Operation Watchtower (Guadalcanal Invasion), so this card begins in the opening Allied hand of cards. The Japanese hand begins with the Savo Island reaction card setting up the potential for the historical opening. This scenario uses the South Pacific mini-map, is four game turns long (game turns 3, 4, 5, and 6) and begins on game turn 3 at the beginning of the Offensives Phase with the US having the option to use their Future Offensive card to pre-empt the Japanese going first. There is no Reinforcement, Replacement, or Strategic Warfare Segments conducted for game turn 3. Victory is determined by the VP schedule outlined in the rulebook. The async mode it is an experimental feature that allow experienced players play faster, if they live in different time zones. Some interactions will be automatically applied if it is possible. If you choose you SHOULD check opponent moves and log before your action. Important things could be missed! What optimized:

* Special reaction rolls automatically.

* Reaction roll automatically (if could not possible to play card).

* Reaction step skipped if nothing possible to do.

* Battle hexes checked automatically from A to Z, one by one.

* Loss applied automatically if only one possible unit to assign hit (could be repeated multiple times), or all units could be eliminated.

* Submarine cards rolled automatically, hits applied automatically if only one unit possible.

* Attrition loss automated.

* Submarine and B29 rolls automated.
